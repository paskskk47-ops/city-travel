About Kerala

Kerala, nestled in the southwestern tip of India, is a land of extraordinary natural beauty. From the pristine backwaters of Alleppey to the mist-covered peaks of Munnar, from the sun-kissed beaches of Kovalam to the spice gardens of Wayanad — Kerala offers a travel experience unlike any other.

Often called "God's Own Country," Kerala is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, Ayurvedic wellness traditions, diverse wildlife, and some of the most spectacular landscapes in Asia. The state's cuisine, classical arts like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and its unique houseboat experience make it a must-visit destination for travelers from around the world.

Kerala Travel Tips

How to Reach:

Cochin International Airport is the main gateway. Trains to Cochin / Trivandrum from all major cities.

Budget:

Budget trips from ₹8,000/person. Standard packages ₹14,000–₹22,000. Luxury packages from ₹35,000+.

What to Pack:

Light cotton clothes for beaches/backwaters. Warm layers for Munnar. Comfortable walking shoes.

Language:

Malayalam is the local language. English and Hindi widely spoken in tourist areas.

How To Reach Kerala

By Air

Cochin International Airport (COK) is the main gateway. Trivandrum (TRV) and Calicut (CCJ) also serve Kerala. Direct flights from all major Indian cities.

By Train

Kerala is well-connected by rail. Ernakulam (Kochi), Trivandrum Central, and Kozhikode are major railway stations with trains from Delhi, Mumbai, and Bengaluru.

By Road

Well-maintained highways connect Kerala to Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh. Private taxis and buses operate from Bengaluru, Chennai, and Coimbatore.

Sample 5-Day Kerala Itinerary

Arrive at Cochin International Airport. Transfer to Munnar (approx. 4–5 hrs). Visit Tea Garden Viewpoint. Hotel check-in and overnight stay.
Visit Eravikulam National Park, Tea Museum, Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, and Top Station. Sunset at a scenic viewpoint.
Drive to Thekkady (3 hrs). Periyar Lake Boat Safari, Spice Plantation visit. Evening cultural show in Thekkady town.
Drive to Alleppey (3 hrs). Board a Traditional Houseboat for an overnight Backwater Cruise. Enjoy local Kerala cuisine on board.
Disembark from houseboat. Shopping in Alleppey. Transfer to Kochi Airport for departure with wonderful memories.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Kerala?
The best time to visit Kerala is from October to March (post-monsoon and winter). This period offers pleasant weather, clear skies, and is ideal for sightseeing, beach activities, and houseboat cruises.
How many days do I need for Kerala?
Ideally 5–7 days to cover the major highlights like Cochin, Alleppey backwaters, and Munnar. You can extend to 10 days to include Thekkady, Wayanad, and Kovalam beach.
Is Kerala good for a honeymoon trip?
Absolutely! Kerala is one of India's most popular honeymoon destinations. The serene backwaters, romantic houseboat stays, hill station charm, and Ayurvedic wellness experiences make it perfect for couples.
