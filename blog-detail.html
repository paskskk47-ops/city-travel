Top 10 Places to Visit in Kerala for First-Time Travelers in 2026

By Sneha Pillai June 15, 2026 8 min read 14 Comments

Kerala, fondly known as "God's Own Country," is a mesmerizing paradise nestled in the southwestern tip of India. With its emerald backwaters, rolling tea-covered hills, pristine beaches, and vibrant culture, planning your first trip here can feel overwhelming because there is simply so much to see!

Whether you are seeking a romantic honeymoon escape, an adventurous family holiday, or a rejuvenating Ayurvedic retreat, Kerala offers a distinct experience for every kind of traveler. To help you craft the perfect itinerary, we have curated the definitive list of the top places you absolutely must include on your maiden voyage to Kerala.

1. Alleppey (Alappuzha) – The Venice of the East

No trip to Kerala is complete without cruising through the tranquil backwaters of Alleppey on a traditional wooden houseboat (Kettuvallam). As you drift along palm-fringed canals, you will witness authentic village life, emerald paddy fields, and colorful kingfishers diving into the water.

  • Best Experience: Book an overnight houseboat stay with freshly prepared traditional Kerala cuisine (don't miss the Karimeen Pollichathu / Pearl Spot fish!).
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March when the weather is cool and breezy.
  • Ideal Duration: 1 to 2 days.

2. Munnar – Rolling Hills of Tea Gardens

Perched at 1,600 meters in the Western Ghats, Munnar is India's premier tea-growing region. The landscape is a breathtaking tapestry of manicured green tea estates shrouded in mist, cascading waterfalls, and colonial-era bungalows.

"Walking through the misty tea gardens of Munnar in the early morning feels like stepping into a living postcard. The aroma of fresh tea leaves and spices in the cool mountain air is unforgettable."

Make sure to visit the Eravikulam National Park to spot the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and stop by the Tea Museum to understand how your morning brew is cultivated and processed.

3. Fort Kochi – Where History & Culture Meet

As the historic port city that welcomed spice traders from across the globe for centuries, Fort Kochi is a melting pot of Portuguese, Dutch, British, and Chinese influences. Stroll along the coastline to see the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets silhouetted against the sunset.

Do not leave without attending an evening performance of Kathakali, Kerala's classical dance-drama renowned for its intricate face make-up, elaborate costumes, and expressive storytelling.

4. Thekkady (Periyar) – Wildlife & Spice Plantations

Home to the renowned Periyar National Park and Tiger Reserve, Thekkady is a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Take a scenic boat safari on Periyar Lake where you can often spot herds of wild elephants, sambar deer, and bison drinking at the water's edge.

Thekkady is also famous for its fragrant spice gardens. Take a guided tour through plantations growing cardamom, pepper, cinnamon, clove, and nutmeg — and pick up some organic spices directly from the farmers!

5. Wayanad – Untamed Nature & Waterfalls

If you prefer raw, untouched wilderness over bustling tourist hubs, Wayanad is your sanctuary. Famous for its prehistoric Edakkal Caves, the soaring Chembra Peak (with a heart-shaped lake!), and the thundering Soochipara Waterfalls, Wayanad is an adventurer's delight.

6. Kovalam & Varkala – Sun, Sand & Ayurveda

When it's time to unwind, head to Kerala's golden coast. Kovalam features crescent-shaped beaches with calm waters perfect for swimming, while Varkala is famous for its dramatic red cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea, lined with bohemian cafes and yoga retreats.

Pro Tips for First-Time Visitors

Travel Smart: While English and Hindi are widely understood in tourist centers, learning a few words in Malayalam like "Namaskaram" (Hello) and "Nanni" (Thank You) will win over the warm-hearted locals instantly!
  • Pack Light Cotton Clothes: Kerala has a tropical climate; breathable cotton wear is essential for coastal areas, while a light jacket is recommended for Munnar.
  • Try Ayurvedic Massage: Book an authentic Ayurvedic spa treatment at a government-certified wellness center to rejuvenate your body and mind.
  • Book Packages Early: During peak season (December–January), houseboats and premium resorts get sold out months in advance.
